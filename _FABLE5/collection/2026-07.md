### 4. Inference candidates (each needs its base value + one falsifier in the issue)

1. **THE LEAD — bifurcation, not boom.** The papers describe luxury "spreading" while construction
   "slows dramatically." Our data reconciles the contradiction: the whole market is repricing
   down; the top tier is simply falling least, and the record prints are thin-sample mix effects
   riding cash-buyer resilience. Luxury isn't diffusing outward from Naples so much as the rest of
   the market is receding from underneath it. [INFERENCE] Falsifier: if bottom-tier YoY declines
   converge toward top-tier declines over the next 2–3 monthly ZHVI updates, the gap is closing
   and the thesis dies.
2. **FMB re-capitalization arc** — private capital, public money, and record recovery coverage in
   the same month, on an island where we hold true-ZIP (33931) data.
3. **Access repricing** — if AA at APF closes AND ULCC RSW routes stick, luxury demand gets a
   structural access upgrade. Watch, don't call.
4. **CRE cross-currents as the same story** — retail tight (people are here, spending), office and
   industrial softening (business expansion cooling), medical office chasing rooftops north. One
   region, one migration wave, read across four sectors.

### 6. Editor's additions to the plan (07/16/2026 — operator handed the pen: "add anything")

Devices I'm adding to the issue architecture. Each one is cheap with the estate we hold and
expensive for anyone without it — the format IS the moat, demonstrated.

- **Issue 001, numbered and dated.** Limited edition means limited: masthead carries "Issue No.
  001 · July 2026 · SWFL", and the canonical page never changes after send. First copies are
  collectible or they're just email.
- **"The Tape"** — a Harper's-Index-style column: ~12–15 one-line numbers, every one sourced from
  the lake or a named outlet, zero commentary. The authority device: the numbers alone, cold.
  Compose day pulls these from brain anchors, so the column is deterministic and lint-proof.
- **"What the papers said / what the ledger says"** — standing fact-check franchise. Take the
  month's loudest press claim and run it against our data. July's installment is already written
  in the wild: "luxury spreading" (press) vs bifurcation (tier data). This section is the moat
  dramatized, monthly.
- **"One Community, By Name"** — debut the neighborhood grain: profile ONE named subdivision per
  issue (median just value, home counts by type, amenities/fees where held). July candidate: pick
  a pair that personifies the split — one entry-tier community (Lehigh/Cape) against one top-tier
  (Naples core), same month, same format. Nobody else writes at this grain.
- **"The Watch"** — dated forward calendar with skin in the game: Marco Hilton decision 07/31, AA
  at APF done-deal moment, FMB resort construction milestones. Each entry states what we expect
  and what would prove us wrong. Issue 002 grades Issue 001's watch, in print.
- **The falsifier ledger (the Nature move).** Every [INFERENCE] in the issue is preregistered:
  base value, direction, falsifier, grade-by date. Next issue opens by grading the last one —
  including the misses. No property publication grades itself; that habit alone separates us from
  the newsletter swamp.
- **The closer, reframed: receipts, not benefits.** Don't LIST key benefits — count them. Close
  with the issue's own audit: N numbers stated, M sources named, zero invented, four lanes
  (our data, your documents, the named web, your own figures), any grain from county to a named
  subdivision. "Everything above was built the way your emails and reports get built." The issue
  is the demo; the closer just turns the camera around.
- **Voice calibration (for the author charge):** Thompson supplies the pace and the verbs;
  Nature supplies the numbers. Drama lives in sentence rhythm, never in the figures — a 2x tier
  gap is stated plainly BECAUSE it's big (real authorities don't hype extremes, they state them).
  Chronicle-length paragraphs, ATTOM/CoStar-grade property vocabulary, zero hedging on computed
  facts, mandatory falsifiers on projections.
- **One-thesis discipline:** the issue holds ONE thesis (bifurcation) and at most two sub-threads
  (FMB re-capitalization, access repricing). Everything else demotes to The Tape or dies. Any
  story that doesn't serve the thesis gets cut no matter how good — it keeps for August.
