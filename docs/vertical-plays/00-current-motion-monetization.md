# Better ways to monetize what we already do — now

**07/10/2026.** The operator asked, alongside "who else can we go after," a sharper question:
*is there a better way to monetize the thing we're already doing?* The thing we're already doing is the
RE brand-injection outreach engine (`scripts/outreach/brand-pilot.mts`). Current model:
**builds are free, the SEND is the paywall** (per-send). Here's the honest read on that and the
alternatives, best-first.

## The core problem with per-send

Per-send meters the *cheapest* part of the stack. Delivering an email costs us fractions of a cent
(Resend). The value we create is the **content** — fresh, local, cited, auto-generated from data no
competitor has assembled for Lee/Collier. Charging for the send:

- caps revenue at volume × margin-on-a-commodity,
- adds friction to the exact action we want customers to take more of,
- and prices the product as "an email tool" — where Mailchimp/Constant Contact/HubSpot already win on
  mechanics and we have zero edge.

We should charge for the content and the intelligence, not the pipe.

## Better motions, ranked

**1. Reframe the asset as horizontal, not RE. (Highest leverage, lowest new build.)**
The engine isn't a real-estate tool — it's a *licensed-professional branded-content outreach engine*.
Swap the DBPR agent list for FL DFS (insurance) or NMLS (mortgage) and the same code runs a new
vertical. One build, many markets. This is worth more than any pricing change to the RE motion alone —
see the board. **Action:** prove the swap on one non-RE licensee list.

**2. Per-seat subscription for the agent, not per-send.**
Flat monthly for "your always-fresh local-market content engine — unlimited sends." Predictable MRR,
higher LTV, removes send friction, and reprices us as intelligence not postage. This is the same move
the whole board is built on; apply it to the motion we already have live.

**3. Brokerage / team white-label — one contract, many seats.**
Sell the office, not the agent. A 40-agent brokerage buys one branded content engine for the whole
team (B2B2C). Higher ACV, far lower churn, and it matches how brokerages already budget (recruiting +
retention tools). The brand-injection machinery is *already* per-brokerage — this is the natural shape.

**4. The list itself is a product.**
We scrape + confirm the DBPR licensee/corp universe for Lee+Collier
(`fixtures/real-estate-brands/dbpr-all-corps-lee-collier.json`, `agents.json`). Enriched, deduped,
brand-tagged agent/brokerage intelligence is a recruiting-intel product brokerages pay for directly —
independent of whether anyone ever sends an email.

**5. Done-for-you managed campaigns (agency retainer).**
For agents who won't self-serve, we run the campaigns for a monthly retainer. Highest touch, highest
price, lowest scale — a revenue floor and a proof-of-value channel, not the main motion.

**6. "Powered by" data licensing / embed.**
License the ZIP-level intelligence to embed in an agent's own site or CRM. Longer sales cycle; park
until a partner asks.

## Recommendation

Two moves, in parallel, both low-build:

- **Reframe + prove the horizontal swap (#1)** — this is where the outsized upside is. The board's
  Tier-A insurance/mortgage plays are literally "run the engine we have against a different list."
- **Flip the RE motion from per-send to per-seat or per-brokerage (#2/#3)** — stop taxing the send;
  charge for the intelligence.

Keep #4 (list-as-product) in the back pocket as a fast cash lane that needs no email at all.

## Open verification before pitching any of this

- Confirm the send-side economics (Resend cost per send) so per-seat pricing has a real floor.
- Confirm what the DBPR corp/agent universe actually contains for a clean "list as product" claim.
- The email-acquisition gap (see board) applies here too — the RE engine's reach is list-limited the
  same way the new verticals are.
