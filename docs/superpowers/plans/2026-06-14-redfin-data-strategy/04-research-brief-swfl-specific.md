# Research Brief: SWFL-Specific Housing Dynamics

## Context

Southwest Florida (Lee + Collier counties) has structural dynamics that make national housing models misleading:
- Extreme snowbird seasonality (Oct–Apr peak, May–Sep trough)
- Post-Ian (Sept 2022) distortion — mass delistings, insurance-driven exits, rebuild lag
- Heavy investor/iBuyer presence in Cape Coral canal-front market
- Luxury/waterfront segment completely decoupled from starter market
- HOA + condo special assessment crisis (post-Surfside law)

## What We Want to Know

1. **Post-Ian housing market research** — what has been published on Ian's effect on Lee County housing prices, insurance, and inventory? Timeline of recovery? Any academic work?

2. **Florida HOA/condo special assessment crisis** — how is the new Florida condo inspection law (SB 4D) affecting the condo segment specifically? Volume of delistings attributable to special assessments?

3. **Insurance-driven delistings** — is there research on homes being delisted due to insurance non-renewal or premium shock rather than market conditions? Florida specific.

4. **Snowbird market seasonality** — published seasonal adjustment factors for Sun Belt retirement markets? How do NAR/Redfin handle Florida seasonality in their metrics?

5. **Canal-front micro-market** — Cape Coral has 400+ miles of canals. Is there research on how waterfront access premiums behave in distress cycles? Gulf-access vs freshwater?
