# Research Brief: AI + Housing Data Products — What's Working

## What We're Building

SWFL Data Gulf is a hyperlocal intelligence platform for Lee/Collier FL. We have:
- 7 years of ZIP-level Redfin seller stress signals (price drops, cancellations, delistings)
- Full housing market tracker at ZIP + city + neighborhood grain
- Flood risk (NFIP AAL per ZIP), permit activity, CRE, economic data
- A brain/pack architecture that synthesizes all signals into grounded direction calls

We're exploring what product features built on top of this data would be most valuable and novel.

## What We Want to Know

1. **Best AI-powered housing data products in 2025–2026** — what are startups building on public real estate data + AI? What's getting traction? Particularly:
   - Negotiation coaches / offer advisors
   - Market timing signals / regime classifiers
   - Seller distress / motivated seller finders
   - Neighborhood fingerprinting / micro-market analysis
   - Early warning systems for price corrections

2. **Investor + institutional tools** — what are hedge funds, iBuyers, and institutional buyers using for signal? Any open-source or published approaches?

3. **Local market AI** — hyperlocal AI products (city/ZIP level) vs national platforms. What does hyper-local enable that national can't?

4. **Redfin's own AI** — what has Redfin published about their AI/ML work on housing data? Their research blog, GitHub, or papers?

5. **Novel product ideas from research** — what have academics proposed that hasn't been productized yet?
